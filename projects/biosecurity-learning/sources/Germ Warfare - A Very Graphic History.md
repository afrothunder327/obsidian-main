## Notes

- The Mongols would fling bodies carrying the plagues over the walls of places they were attacking. 
- Germany tried to develop biological weapons that targeted horses during World War I. Horses were targeted because they were key to military transport.
- After the war in 1925 many countries signed the [[Geneva Protocol]] prohibiting the use of many toxins and germs in warfare. However, Japan never signed, and indeed, they went on to develop a large biological weapons programs on the basis that a treaty banning them suggesting that they had large potential.
- In 1969, Nixon announced the end of the US bioweapons program. 
- The UN Bioweapons convention was signed in 1972. Nixon widely disparaged the treaty for being weak. It had no enforcement or verification mechanism, unlike its nuclear counterparts. The USSR flagrantly flouted it.
