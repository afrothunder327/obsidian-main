## Notes

- They cite this [NeurIPS paper](https://neurips.cc/virtual/2025/loc/san-diego/131277) to claim that models are increasingly capable of agentically manipulating biological AI models. 
	- The evidence appears pretty mixed but obviously this will be a growing concern.
	
- They got a non-expert to fine-tune Evo 2 on sequeucne of human-infecting viruses using Claude Code. The non-expert was able to do so in a week without encountering any safety refusals.

Three priorities from work:
1. Data filtering is not enough as a safeguard. It is easy to bypass. We need trusted-access programs.
2. LLM agent developers should test how good their models are at agentically using BAIMs. This includes BAIM modification *and* creation.
3. Policymakers should invest in physical chokepoints like DNA synthesis screening.
