## Notes

How [[CAR-T therapy|CAR-T therapies]] work:
- A patient's [[T cells]] are extracted, and then sent to a facility at which they are re-engineered to bind to specific [[Protein|proteins]] on cancerous cells.
- After a course of chemotherapy, they are reintroduced into the patient's body, where they seeking out cells carrying the target protein and destroy them.
- Because they are living cells, they can proliferate within the host body for months or years.
![[CAR-T therapy.png]]


There are currently Two approved [[CAR-T therapy|CART therapies]] approved in the US. Both treat bone cancer and target the same surface antigen on myeloma cells.

The Chinese therapy is basically the superior treatment. Higher long-term survival rates, as seen below.

![[Myeloma immunotherapy survival rates.png]]


China is now developing more cell therapies than the US.
