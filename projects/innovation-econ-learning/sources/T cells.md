[T cells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T_cell) or T lymphocytes are a class of [[Lymphocytes|lymphocytes]] characterized by their possession of a [T-cell receptor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T-cell_receptor). They are primarily developed in the bone marrow.

There are many different types of T cells. Claude describes their primary functions as:
- **[[Helper T cells]]** coordinate immune response by releasing signaling molecules ([[Cytokines|cytokines]]) that activate killer T cells, stimulate B cells to produce antibodies, and recruit other immune cells.
- **[[Cytotoxic T cells]]** directly destroy cells that are infected by viruses, are cancerous, or otherwise damages. It recognizes these conditions via abnormal [[Antigens|antigens]] on their surfacee.
- **[[Regulatory T cells]]** modulate immune response to prevent overreaction and autoimmunity.
- **[[Memory T cells]]** persist after an infection clears, enabling faster immune response if the infection is recontracted.
