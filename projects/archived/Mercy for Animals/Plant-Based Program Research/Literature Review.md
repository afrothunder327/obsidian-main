### [Students' Attitudes Toward Meatless Meals in School Lunches](https://www.jneb.org/article/S1499-4046(17)30460-8/fulltext)
- Same as above, were given printed information on plant-based diets and some promotion during lunchtime.
- "Most students did not recognize beans as a good substitute for meat either pre- or post-intervention (elementary: pre-28.8% vs. post-28.5%; middle: 28.6% vs. 26.8%). There was a significant increase after the campaign (51.7% to 88.9%) in elementary students who reported hearing of the “meatless meals” campaign but a significant decline (26.1% vs. 21.4%) in students agreeing that eating meatless once a week would be good for their health."
- "Middle school students showed no significant change in any parameter measured, and the majority of all students reported no change in eating behaviors." - would be so nice to see these studies.
- The campaign increased awareness among elementary students about “meatless meals” but had no positive effect on other knowledge or behavior for any students.

### [Analyzing the Effectiveness of a Meatless Monday Intervention on Meat Consumption and Associated Pro-Environmental Spillover Behavior Throughout the Week](https://kb.osu.edu/bitstream/handle/1811/91668/MeatlessMondayBehavioralSpillover.pdf?sequence=1&isAllowed=y)
- "This study addressed such research gaps by testing whether an intervention encouraging participation in MM would lead to significantly reduced meat consumption on Monday and during the rest of a given week, as well as to higher performance rates of other PEBs during that week (i.e., behavioral spillover). We hypothesized that each effect would be observed."
- Got treatment and control group. Experimental groups were given flyers encouraging participation in meatless mondays. 
- "Subsequent analyses led to all three hypotheses being rejected. In fact, the experimental group reported higher week-level meat consumption, potentially due to failed baseline randomization."
- "it does not appear that any experimental research has been conducted to ascertain the specific impacts that the Meatless Monday campaign has on actual individual meat consumption behavior (e.g., in the form of food diaries)."
