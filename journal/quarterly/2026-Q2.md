## STEP 5: SANITY CHECKING

### Question 1: Future Quarters
**Is there anything you want to do in future quarters that you aren't going to do now?**

This is a good place to save notes for your next quarterly reviews. Maybe some rocks will trigger next rocks or maybe some goals will trigger goals for next quarter.


### Question 2: Time Allocation
**How much time will each rock and pebble take? Is there enough time to do all of these rocks? Is it realistic?**

If it's not realistic, go back and revise. Consider using a weekly time use calculator to ensure that everything you are doing can fit into your schedule.


### Question 3: Slack & Flexibility
**Slack: Do your commitments above give you the flexibility and freedom to respond to (inevitable) unanticipated projects or issues, or are you at max capacity?**

Consider using a weekly time use calculator to ensure that everything you are doing can fit into your schedule. (Aim for ~20% buffer capacity)


### Question 4: Systems Review

**What is your system going to be to make sure you stick to these rocks?**

I know that each rock has a bit on how you will ensure they will be achieved, but I suggest reflecting a bit more on your overall system and potentially revisiting these. (Feel free to copy this from a prior review as hopefully this isn't something that changes much. If you already have these, it's still good to reflect upon them for a minute.)

**Things to consider:**

- **Review & Tracking:** How will you review and track each goal you've set? How often? (e.g., weekly, bi-weekly)

- **Habits & Routines:** Do you have any habits and/or routines that are important for achieving your goals? Do you have a good way of ensuring you'll do that? (Don't go too overboard here as complex habits are hard to maintain.)

- **Daily Planning:** Do you have a way to plan each day to ensure it is focused on furthering your goals? Do you have a good way to ensure you do your plan each day?

- **Dashboard & Metrics:** Do you need a dashboard to track key metrics? How will you ensure you look at it and keep it up to date?

- **Capture & Processing System:** Do you have a good general system for capturing and processing things that come up? Does that system flow well? Is it stale? Is it reliable?

- **Task Management:** Where do your tasks live? Does that task system flow well? Is it reliable? Are your tasks in service of your rocks?

- **Accountability:** Do you have good ways to create external accountability around your rocks?

- **System Pain Points:** What bugs you the most about your current system? What's not working? What would be the single most important change?

**Important Note:** Don't go too overboard with investing in a ton of system changes at once! It will be too much change and overwhelming. Start gradually and change fewer things. Make a note to follow up on other things later. Changing too many things at once is the most common mistake! Besides adopting this quarterly planning, make just one other change and make a note to follow up on the rest next quarter.

## 7-POMODORO FAST-TRACK GUIDE

**Aim for completion in 7 × 25-minute Pomodoros.** Be concise, trust your gut, and aim for good enough rather than perfect. Skip optional sections if needed.

| Pomodoro | Content |
|----------|---------|
| **P1 & P2** | Step 1 - Reflection (including Quick Check ratings) |
| **P3** | Step 2 (Vision) + Step 3 (Combine & Refine - Initial List) |
| **P4** | Step 3 (Distill List) + Step 4 (Most Important Rock, including If-Then plans) |
| **P5** | Step 4 (Detail Rocks 2 & 3, including If-Then plans) |
| **P6** | Step 4 (Pebbles + Anti-Rocks) |
